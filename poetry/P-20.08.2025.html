Poetry

I don’t want half love

Published: 20th August 2025

I’ve always been a girl that believes in everything or nothing,
Maybe it’s because I grew up in a home where love was never in between,
I love my mother and my step father
And they loved each other,
Until one day they didn’t,
And my sister and I would sit halfway down the steps,
And listen to them learning to unlove each other,
I learned to fall asleep to the sound of raised voices and breaking dishes,
They’re getting a divorce when I’m 7, 12, 17,
But the only consistency is screaming,
And silence only broken by slamming doors,
Bits of my parents love dances between the tension of my childhood home,
And my first place alone,
Like dust that settles on picture frames that hold their wedding photo,
They say a quiet love is safe,
But I seek out a love that shouts,
I hate the in betweens of hellos and goodbyes,
Moving from one place to another,
The space between I love you and I can’t do this anymore,
I want what lasts forever though I’m not foolish enough to think that anything does,
But I am certain that my mother once meant it when she said I do,
So don’t try to love me unless you believe you really could,
If you can’t promise to stay,
When the house shakes so hard that’s our wedding photo falls off the wall,
Please don’t ask me to open my door,
I don’t want pieces of your love,
And my half-built half-broken home,
And I don’t want my daughters sitting on the steps,
Listening to us yell,
Wondering if love is supposed to sound like this,
They say the best loves are patient,
That they’re quiet,
But love doesn’t have to be silent,
And silence doesn’t have to mean absence,
It could be my step fathers gentle hand on the small of my mothers back,
Or the soft crackle of the fire he built for her to read by,
Warmly,
Calmly,
And loving someone loudly doesn’t have to mean screaming,
It could be my parents laughter spilling into the night,
Reaching the ears of a little girl,
Who doesn’t yet know that her heart is listening,
Through walls she is unaware that she is building,
It’s in the in-between that she discovers the secret to what holds a home together,
So I’ll build my home with you,
In the hush after the hurt,
In the fireplace that always stays warm,
And I will teach our daughters that everything or nothing,
Is not the only way to love.
