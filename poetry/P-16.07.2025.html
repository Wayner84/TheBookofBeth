Poetry

Double Meaning

Published: 16th July 2025

I love plates that are bowls too,
Ain’t it great that they hold soup,
Am I late,
Are they old news,
Or do you like plates that are bowls too,
See,
Breakfast lunch and dinner,
I’m always munching a winner,
Such fun,
What a thriller,
A plate with the sides up,
Because I’m a spiller,
For a big lunch,
Like a weekend roast,
Or a quick brunch,
Beans on toast,
It fits tonnes of porridge oats,
And whatever floats your boat,
Like winter food,
If I’m feeling homely,
A bit of soup,
Minestrone,
Weetabix,
With a bit of oatly,
2 biscuits,
3 if I’m feeling lonely,
Once you try,
You can’t forget it,
They’re easy to hold if your palms are sweaty,
Lightweight,
If knees are weak and arms are heavy,
I got it on my sweater,
Mum’s spaghetti,

They suit any meal,
I’ll tell you that for starters,
So fetch your bechamel sauce and passata,
My knees are weak as I unpack the larder,
I got it on my sweater,
Dads lasagna,
I like plates that are bowls too,
Ain’t it great that they hold soup,
Am I late or are they old news,
Do you like plates that are bowls too,
See,
Tall bowls are precarious,
The reasons why; they’re various,
All told,
They don’t stack well,
And,
They lack the surface area,
Straight plates are not too easy,
Your food better not move too freely,
You’ll be fetching napkins if your risotto is too creamy,

But the bowl plate,
It’s got style,
It’s got grace in its own way,
Holds dhal,
Holds cake,
Holds sorbet,
Keeps a smile on my face for a whole day,
Fruit salad on a plate,
I’m getting five a day,
Forgetting all my pain,
Spaghetti bolognese,
I’m taking dins to unassailable heights,
Creating things others fail all the time,
Up till 10,
I could make this all night,
Uncle bens microwaveable rice,
Yeah I like plates that are bowls too,
Ain’t it great that they hold soup,
Am I late or are they old news,
Do you like plates that are bowls too?
